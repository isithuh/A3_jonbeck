The story so far

Jon picked up his first camera as a teenager in regional Queensland, photographing the sheds, saltpans and wide horizons within riding distance of home. Fifteen years on, the same curiosity still sets the itinerary: from Australia’s red soil centre to its working harbours, and to the faces of the people he meets along the way.

His work moves across five collections: portraits, seascapes, landscapes, buildings and street. What binds them is an eye for the moment a familiar scene turns unfamiliar. Every photograph tells a story, but with a difference.

Jon’s prints have earned international awards and coverage in the editorial press. Each photograph is released as a strictly limited edition of 50, 100 or 250, hand-printed on museum-grade Fujiflex Crystal Archive paper and signed with a certificate of authenticity.

When he isn’t printing, Jon is teaching. His small-group workshops hand-lead photographers of every level through some of Australia’s most photographed regions, from wild Tasmania to the goldfield streets of Ballarat.
